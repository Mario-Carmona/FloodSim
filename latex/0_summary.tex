\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

\onehalfspacing

This Final Master’s Project presents FloodSim, a distributed, high-performance flood simulation framework developed in C++ to address 
the critical computational latency of traditional hydrodynamic forecasting models during flash flood events. Sparked by the catastrophic 
2024 DANA crisis in Valencia, Spain, this research bridges the gap between complex physical hydraulic routing and real-time emergency 
response infrastructure.

The proposed system leverages a multi-dimensional, multilayer Cellular Automata ($m:n-\text{CA}^k$) engine to model surface water flow 
dynamics over discretized spatial grids using continuous GIS-derived topography and roughness datasets. To prevent the architectural 
rigidity common in scientific software, the system implements a decoupled Hexagonal Architecture that completely isolates core hydraulic 
transition physics from data ingestion modules. Furthermore, a distributed telemetry layer powered by the MQTT publish-subscribe protocol 
is incorporated, enabling sub-second concurrent synchronization with remote 3D visualization platforms and field sensors to construct a 
Reactive Digital Twin.

The framework was experimentally validated by reconstructing the 2024 Valencia flood across a 1,311 km$^2$ domain, focusing on the heavily 
impacted sectors of Pla de Quart and Paiporta. The simulation successfully captured non-linear floodwave propagation and maximum inundation 
depths within seconds, proving that discrete parallelized architectures can achieve finite-volume accuracy without the immense computational 
overhead. Ultimately, FloodSim demonstrates the viability of distributed cellular models as practical, real-time decision-support tools 
capable of mitigating modern socio-environmental crises and saving lives.
